12 Jyotirlingas: Pillars of Divine Light

Where Shiva manifests as columns of effulgent light, blessing devotees with eternal grace.

Sacred Abodes

Somnath (Gujarat)

The first among the twelve, where the Moon God worshipped Shiva to regain his lustre. Destroyed and rebuilt several times, it stands as a symbol of resilience.

Mallikarjuna (Andhra Pradesh)

Located on Srisailam mountain, this is where Shiva and Parvati are worshipped together, representing the union of masculine and feminine energies.

Mahakaleshwar (Madhya Pradesh)

The only south-facing Jyotirlinga, known for its unique Bhasma Aarti where sacred ashes are used to worship the deity.

Omkareshwar (Madhya Pradesh)

Located on an island shaped like the sacred 'Om' symbol, this lingam represents the primal sound of the universe.

Kedarnath (Uttarakhand)

Perched in the Himalayas at 3,583 meters, this remote temple is part of the Char Dham pilgrimage and remains covered in snow for six months.

Bhimashankar (Maharashtra)

Nestled in the Sahyadri hills, this temple is associated with the legend of Shiva destroying the demon Tripurasura.

The twelve Jyotirlingas are sacred shrines where Shiva is worshipped in his form as a pillar of light. Each has unique legends, architectural styles, and spiritual significance, forming a network of powerful energy centers across India.
