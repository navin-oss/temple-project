Char Dham Yatra: Divine Sojourn

Embark on a spiritual journey to the abodes of the divine.

The Sacred Sanctuaries

Yamunotri

The origin of the Yamuna River and the seat of Goddess Yamuna.

Gangotri

The source of the Ganges River and the shrine of Goddess Ganga.

Kedarnath

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, located in the Himalayas.

Badrinath

The abode of Lord Vishnu, located between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges.

The Char Dham Yatra is a pilgrimage to four holy sites in Uttarakhand, India. These shrines are dedicated to various deities and are considered highly sacred by Hindus. The yatra is believed to cleanse the soul and bestow spiritual merit.

Visual Pilgrimage

The Char Dham Yatra is not only a spiritual journey but also a visual spectacle. The temples are nestled amidst breathtaking Himalayan scenery, with rivers, mountains, and lush greenery adding to their divine beauty.
